The Inspiration

IT STARTED WITH MY GRANDFATHERS.

Growing up, some of my favorite moments were listening to my grandfathers tell sports stories. Games they attended, moments they witnessed, teams they loved. Those stories shaped me as a fan.

But over time, I noticed something. The details were fading. The score. The opponent. Who was there with them. What the weather was like. The little things that make a memory feel real — slowly going quiet.

Those stories deserved better. And so do yours.

One day, your kids and grandkids are going to ask you what it was like to be there. I Was There makes sure you'll have the answer.

The Mission

KEEP THE MEMORIES FROM FADING.

I spent a month designing I Was There to capture everything a sports memory is made of — not just the final score, but the atmosphere, the weather, who you were with, what you ate, the moments that made you leap out of your seat.

Because being a sports fan isn't just about watching games. It's about the stories you carry forward and the ones you pass down.

This journal is for every fan who has ever sat in a stadium, a living room, or a bar and thought: I never want to forget this.
